Primary source documents are preserved and accessible through the Department of Special Collections at the University of California, Santa Barbara (UCSB) Library.

SBHC Mss 113

Pacific Pride Foundation Records

Location: Box 12 | Series 3: Albums and Photographs

The Pacific Pride Foundation (originally the Western Addiction Services Program, later the Gay & Lesbian Resource Center) has served the Santa Barbara and Central Coast communities since the mid-1970s. This collection contains photographs, scrapbooks, local directories, newsletters, and mutual aid records documenting local community organization and critical care networks during the HIV/AIDS crisis.

Mss 333

Lois Capps papers

Location: Box 15

Lois Capps served in the U.S. House of Representatives from 1998 to 2017, representing California's Central Coast. Her papers document federal legislative debates, public correspondence, military advocacy (e.g. 'Don't Ask, Don't Tell' reports), local UCSB student organization records, and same-sex marriage debates in California.

ARC Mss 95

Bill Berkowitz DataCenter Collection

Location: Box 4 | Bill Berkowitz DataCenter and right-wing movements collection

Bill Berkowitz is an investigative journalist who researched conservative political groups. This collection contains extensive media clippings, television advertising analyses, flyers, and publications tracking anti-gay campaigns, moral panic campaigns, conversion therapy ads, and media controversies surrounding gay rights during the late 20th century.
